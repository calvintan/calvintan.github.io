Design Faster: 10 Figma Tips for Designers

26 Sep 2023

Have you ever had those moments in Figma where you feel like you're moving in slow motion, clicking through layers and components, knowing deep down that you can work faster and smarter? We've all experienced those pangs of frustration, realizing there's room for improvement.

In this concise guide, I've curated 10 practical tips to elevate your efficiency in Figma. Whether you're just starting your journey or aiming to sharpen your skills, these insights will smooth out your design process.

1. Select an element with one click

Here is a typical scenario. To adjust a single object that is nested deep inside of a component, you end up clicking endlessly through each layer to select this element.

Do this instead: Click on the object while holding ⌘cmd, and you'll be able to select the object with one click! This is also known as a "deep select".

2. Select several layers

Do you need to selectively edit several objects in a component? Hold ⌘cmd + ⇧shift and click on each object. Mis-clicked an object? Simply click on it again to deselect it.

3. Select children or parent layers

When objects are nested inside a frame or group, the frame layer is called the "parent" and the layers inside are called the "children". We can click on the parent layer and press ↩return to select all of the children layers.

Pressing ⇧shift + ↩return (or the \ key) does the opposite and selects the parent layer. I use these shortcuts frequently when modifying the contents of a table. First I will click on the desired table column, press ↩return to select all the cells of that column, and modify all of them at once!

4. Select sibling layers

Children objects are called "siblings" of each other, and we can select sibling layers too. Press tab to select the first sibling layer, and press tab again to select the next sibling layer. You can also press ⇧shift + tab to select the previous sibling layer.

You can now navigate up and down the Layers panel with just your keyboard!

5. Collapse all layers

Your Layers panel will start to get untidy as you are busy editing objects, leaving a trail of "unfolded" layers. Pressing ⌥option + l will collapse the layers back into a neat stack.

Using this shortcut regularly will ensure your Layers panel is kept tidy at all times, making it easy to sift through to find any layer.

6. Duplicating objects

There are several ways to duplicate an object, choose the method that best suits you!

  1. Copy and paste the object in place by using ⌘cmd + c and ⌘cmd + v. Alternatively ⌘cmd + d does the same thing by pressing just 1 shortcut.
  2. Click and drag the object while holding ⌥option. Drag while holding both ⌥option + shift to create a duplicate along the horizontal or vertical axis.

7. Add multiple images

Need to add multiple pictures into your design? ⌘cmd + ⇧shift + k is your go-to shortcut to add images in bulk. Select your image files after pressing the shortcut, then click to place them on the canvas or on an object.

8. Crop an image

There is no need to crop an image from the Design panel. Hold ⌘cmd and drag on the edge of that image to crop an image quickly. This not only works well with images, but also with frames! Just remember to tick the clip content option in your frame.

9. Copy and paste properties

You might want to reuse styles that you've applied to an object or layer.

Copy styles by pressing ⌘cmd + option + c. Then select the other objects, and press ⌘cmd + option + v to paste.

10. Copy and paste properties from the Design panel

A bonus tip: you can copy a style by clicking on the left edge of a fill, an effect, or even a prototype interaction! Once it is selected (highlighted in blue), you can copy and paste onto any layer to apply the same style.
